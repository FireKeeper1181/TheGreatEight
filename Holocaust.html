Holocaust

Nazi Genocide: The Holocaust was the systematic, state-sponsored persecution and extermination of six million Jews by the Nazi regime and its collaborators during World War II (1939-1945).

Concentration and Death Camps: The Nazis established concentration camps where millions of innocent people, including Jews, Romani people, disabled individuals, and political dissidents, suffered forced labor, starvation, and mass killings.

Final Solution: The "Final Solution" was the Nazis' plan to systematically annihilate the entire Jewish population of Europe. It involved mass shootings, gas chambers, and other forms of mass murder.

Aftermath and Remembrance: The Holocaust is a dark chapter in human history, and its aftermath led to the establishment of international human rights conventions. Holocaust remembrance and education aim to prevent such atrocities from happening again.
