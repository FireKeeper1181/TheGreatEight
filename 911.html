9/11

Terrorist Attacks: On September 11, 2001, a series of coordinated terrorist attacks by the extremist group al-Qaeda targeted the World Trade Center towers in New York City and the Pentagon in Washington, D.C.

Impact and Casualties: The attacks resulted in the deaths of nearly 3,000 people and caused significant economic and infrastructure damage. It marked a pivotal moment in modern history, shaping international relations and security policies.

Global Response: The 9/11 attacks led to a global response against terrorism, with increased security measures and international efforts to combat extremist ideologies.

Memorial and Remembrance: The National September 11 Memorial & Museum in New York City commemorates the lives lost during the attacks, serving as a place for reflection and remembrance.
